Urgent Call

Responding to Haiti’s urgent needs with dignity and discipline.

More than 3,000 children are at risk of educational disruption. Current priorities include preparing or developing five schools, providing hot meals, securing educational materials, supporting displaced families, assisting migrants near border zones, strengthening human rights and prison follow-up, supporting vulnerable prisoners, mobilizing lawyers and volunteers, helping small vendors and women entrepreneurs through microcredit, and improving access to the Grand Gosier road.

Behind every child, there is a future.

Education Support

Behind every child, there is a future.

Education is one of Haiti’s most urgent priorities. America Chaplains International Corp Inc. supports educational continuity, school readiness, hot meals, learning materials, and community-based solutions for children at risk of losing access to school.

Human Rights & Prison Support

Behind every prisoner, there is a human life.

Our human rights and prison support work seeks to protect dignity, encourage justice, and provide compassionate follow-up for vulnerable prisoners and those affected by exclusion or legal insecurity. We welcome support from lawyers, chaplains, volunteers, and institutions committed to humane service.

Displaced Families

We support families affected by gang violence and displacement through accompaniment, practical solidarity, food support, and dignity-centered humanitarian care.

Migrant Support

We recognize the vulnerability of migrants near border zones and work toward compassionate, responsible, and practical support.

Youth & Peacebuilding

We invest in youth as agents of peace, service, moral courage, and community renewal.

Cooperative & Community Recovery

Recovery begins when communities regain the means to move, work, and hope.

Community recovery requires more than emergency relief. It requires access, livelihoods, microcredit, women’s entrepreneurship, local organization, and pathways that help families rebuild economic and social stability.
